## Recommended Naming Convention

Use this format for all new photos:

**`[category]-[subject]-[number].jpg`**

### Examples:
- `hall-main-01.jpg`
- `event-40k-02.jpg`
- `group-playing-05.jpg`
- `painted-army-03.jpg`
- `table-setup-dnd-01.jpg`
- `hobby-night-02.jpg`

### Category Guidelines

| Category          | Use For                                      | Example Filenames                     |
|-------------------|----------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| `hall`            | General shots of the gaming space            | `hall-main-01.jpg`, `hall-wide-02.jpg` |
| `event-warhammer` | Warhammer 40k, Age of Sigmar, Spearhead      | `event-40k-01.jpg`, `event-aos-03.jpg` |
| `event-community` | Open play, Pokémon, Magic, D&D, board games  | `event-pokemon-01.jpg`, `event-dnd-02.jpg` |
| `group-playing`   | **High priority** – Photos with people playing | `group-playing-01.jpg`, `players-04.jpg` |
| `table-setup`     | Nice terrain setups, cool tables (no people) | `table-setup-01.jpg`, `terrain-big-02.jpg` |
| `painted`         | Painted models and armies                    | `painted-40k-01.jpg`, `army-display-03.jpg` |
| `hobby`           | Painting, building, hobby area shots         | `hobby-painting-01.jpg`, `hobby-night-02.jpg` |
| `ribbon-cutting`  | Opening day / special events                 | `ribbon-cutting-01.jpg`                |
| `hero`            | Main homepage background                     | `hero-01.jpg`                          |

## How to Add New Photos

1. **Collect real photos** from your Instagram (`@fenris_gaming_llc`) and Facebook.
2. **Download the highest quality version** possible (originals from your phone are best).
3. **Name the file** using the naming convention above.
4. **Place the file** in the `public/images/` folder.
5. **Tell me** which new photo you added and where you want it to appear (e.g., "Replace the Warhammer photo on the homepage with `event-40k-05.jpg`").

I will then update the website code to use the new image.

---

## Tips for Good Photos

- **Groups of people playing** are currently the highest priority.
- Photos with multiple people at a table look much better than empty tables.
- Good lighting matters — try to avoid very dark or very washed-out shots.
- Painted models look best when photographed on a table with some terrain.
- Vertical photos can work, but horizontal (landscape) shots usually look better in the current layouts.
